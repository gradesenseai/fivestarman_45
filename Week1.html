Fivestarman 45-Day Challenge

Week One

Discussion Guide · Days 1 through 7

A word before we start

Honestly? At first I didn't know if I was a fan of this book. The pointed examples about what to do, what to change, what to buy all felt a little kitschy. But the further I got in, the more I realized that's exactly what we need as men. If we're not given strong examples and asked to make commitments, we have a tendency to not follow through. That's why this is a 45-day challenge. Otherwise it would just be 45 days of reading.

Day 01 · Tuesday May 6

Let's Get Going

Adventurous
Summary

This 45-day journey isn't self-help. It's recalibration. Day 1 sets the foundation: a man's life should be measured against God's standard of excellence, and that begins with stewarding the body He gave you. Paul ran with purpose in every step. The Adventurous Spirit starts with a man willing to get off the sideline and back into training.

Verse

I run straight to the goal with purpose in every step.

1 Corinthians 9:26
Discussion Question

What purposeful steps are you taking to improve your health and fitness?

  • A specific workout schedule the man actually shows up for, not just owns equipment for
  • A wake-up or bedtime change to make morning movement possible
  • Cutting alcohol, sugar, late-night eating, or screen time
  • Getting an overdue physical, bloodwork, or doctor's appointment
  • A walking habit, lifting partner, or accountability check-in
Day 02 · Wednesday May 7

Ignite the Flame

Entrepreneurial
Summary

A man's greatest resource isn't outside him. It's the God-given gift already deposited inside him. Paul told Timothy to fan that gift into flame, not chase what God hadn't given. The Entrepreneurial drive begins with honest inventory: identify what God put in you, then steward it. Long-term provision comes from developing what's already on deposit, not envying what someone else carries.

Verse

Fan into flame the gift of God, which is in you.

2 Timothy 1:6
Discussion Question

What are the 5 things you are good at?

  • Skills people consistently come to you for, even ones you don't think of as "real" skills
  • Things that come naturally and bring joy when you're in the middle of them
  • Talents your spouse, parents, or close friends point out that you tend to dismiss
  • Gifts you used to lean on more before life got busy
  • Things that could be monetized if developed intentionally
Day 03 · Thursday May 8

Dress for Significance

Gallant
Summary

When Joseph was called from the dungeon to stand before Pharaoh, he stopped to shave and change clothes first. The dungeon hadn't defined him, but he wasn't going to walk into the throne room dressed like it either. A Gallant man understands that how he presents himself matters to his wife, his kids, his work, and his witness. He's groomed and dressed appropriately for the occasion God is calling him into.

Verse

So Pharaoh sent for Joseph, and he was quickly brought from the dungeon. When he had shaved and changed his clothes, he came before Pharaoh.

Genesis 41:14
Discussion Question

In what ways is it important to be intentional about your physical appearance?

  • How you present yourself at home, not just outside the house
  • Clean haircut, trimmed beard, fresh shave. The basics of grooming.
  • A closet that fits the man you are today, not three versions ago
  • Having something to wear when the call comes for a funeral, wedding, or important meeting
  • What your daily "uniform" communicates about how seriously you take yourself
Day 04 · Friday May 9

Wise Up

Faithful
Summary

Proverbs was written for one purpose: attaining wisdom and living a disciplined, prudent life. Wisdom isn't just knowing. It's knowing and applying. A Faithful man pursues wisdom intentionally, because good leadership demands it. The day's prescription is concrete: read Proverbs daily, study men who finished well, sit under someone ten years ahead of you, and decide to be a reader. Wisdom is the greatest pursuit of a man's life.

Verse

For attaining wisdom and discipline... for acquiring a disciplined and prudent life.

Proverbs 1:2–3
Discussion Question

What are you doing, or will commit to doing, to gain the wisdom of the Lord?

  • A daily Bible reading plan, in or beyond Proverbs
  • Finishing books you already own instead of buying more
  • A standing time with an older Christian man over coffee, breakfast, or a walk
  • A 30-minute reading block built into the day
  • A specific application target. Where do I already know better than I act?
Day 05 · Saturday May 10

Be a Solution

Philanthropic
Summary

A man was created to solve a problem. His life should be a contribution to the world around him, not a withdrawal from other people's resources. The Philanthropic passion calls a man to look around, find the need, and become the answer. The author lands it with a sentence worth memorizing: "I never ask God to meet the need of someone when I have the ability to meet it myself." Take ownership of a problem, and the creativity to solve it will come.

Verse

The Lord will reward everyone for whatever good he does.

Ephesians 6:8
Discussion Question

What problems or needs of others around you could you be the solution to?

  • Something your wife has been quietly carrying that you have the capacity to take on
  • A neighbor, widow, or older family member with a practical need
  • A man in your circle who's struggling and needs a phone call, not just a prayer
  • A skill you already have that someone else doesn't and would pay (or need) to use
  • A problem in your church or community where you keep saying "someone should..."
Day 06 · Monday May 11

Bad Weather Player

Adventurous
Summary

Ecclesiastes warns the farmer who waits for perfect weather: he'll never plant, and he'll never reap. A man can't only show up on sunny days. He has to condition himself to perform in the wind and the rain too. The Adventurous Spirit refuses to let circumstances beyond his control dictate his behavior. He stops watching the clouds, gets in the game, and trusts God with the harvest.

Verse

Whoever watches the wind will not plant; whoever looks at the clouds will not reap.

Ecclesiastes 11:4
Discussion Question

What can we do to not allow circumstances beyond our control to dictate our behavior?

  • Naming the "cloud" specifically: finances, health, a relationship, perfectionism
  • Identifying what action you'd already be taking if the cloud lifted
  • A standing rhythm (workout, men's group, sabbath) that holds whether circumstances cooperate or not
  • A concrete rule that overrides the excuse, like "I send it after two rewrites" or "I show up even when I don't feel ready"
  • An accountability relationship that calls you out when you start watching the clouds
Day 07 · Tuesday May 12

Define Your Mission

Entrepreneurial
Summary

A mission statement is a GPS for a man's life. Without one, he drifts. Busy but unfocused, working hard but unsure where he's going. The author calls this one of the greatest mistakes men make: never taking the time to define who they are and what they're about. The Entrepreneurial passion demands clarity. Identify your core values, name who you're called to, set long-term and short-term goals, then write a statement clear enough to keep you on course when life pulls hard in every other direction.

Verse

Come, let us build ourselves a city, with a tower that reaches to the heavens, so that we may make a name for ourselves.

Genesis 11:4
Discussion Question

Were you able to come up with a mission statement? What is it?

  • Core values that act as filters: Faith, Family, Integrity, Excellence, Stewardship
  • A clear calling order. Who comes first when demands compete?
  • Long-term goals broken into short-term, bite-sized pieces
  • A statement short enough to remember, specific enough to convict you when you drift
  • Something you can read on the inside cover of your Bible or your phone lock screen

Built from four core values, Faith, Family, Integrity, Excellence, and a clear calling order: family first, then the men I lead and whoever God puts in front of me, with work as the vehicle. The "margin enough to be present, ambition enough to leave a legacy" line is the tension this 45 days is going to hold me to.

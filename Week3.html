Fivestarman 45-Day Challenge

Week Three

Discussion Guide · Days 15 through 21

Guide
A word before we start

Week Three marks the halfway point and the depth charge. Days 15-21 are where the principles start hitting bone—generosity tested, culture confronted, excellence demanded, older men respected, anger mastered, the next generation invested in, and solitude sought. You've built the foundation. Now you're living in it.

Day 15 · Tuesday May 20

Sow A Seed

Philanthropic
Summary

Giving isn't about the size of the gift. It's about the heart behind it. You're planting a seed that will produce a harvest. A farmer doesn't scatter seed carelessly or give reluctantly. The Philanthropic man gives from a confident, considerate, grateful, and generous heart. Take your loose change—money you weren't using anyway—and give it to a worthy cause. The point is turning something worthless into something priceless by giving it with joy.

Verse

God loves a cheerful giver.

2 Corinthians 9:7
Discussion Question

What's one area of your financial giving where you've been acting more out of guilt or habit than genuine joy, and what would it look like to shift that?

  • A tithe or offering that's become obligation instead of conviction
  • Giving to an organization because you should, not because you trust their mission
  • Money given out of comparison ("they have more, so I should give more") instead of gratitude
  • Giving to be noticed or approved, rather than giving with anonymity and quiet joy
  • The guilt-based giving that happens after you say no to something, as a way to manage the guilt
  • A specific organization or cause you actually believe in, trust deeply, and give to with confidence that it's being used well
Day 16 · Wednesday May 21

The Bull Ring

Adventurous
Summary

You're surrounded by an anti-man spirit. It resents authentic male productivity, mocks biblical manhood as archaic, and attacks relentlessly—often so subtly we don't notice. Living as an authentic man is countercultural. The Adventurous Spirit doesn't get sidetracked chasing the fight. Instead, it builds something solid: your walk with God, your family's alignment with His truth. That's the real bull ring. Not spotting the enemy, but standing firm in who you actually are.

Verse

Many bulls surround me; strong bulls of Bashan encircle me.

Psalm 22:12
Discussion Question

Where in your daily life have you noticed the cultural pressure to soften or shrink your sense of manhood, and how have you responded to it?

  • The subtle message that biblical manhood is outdated or harmful
  • Men apologizing for basic leadership, productivity, or strength
  • Being painted as "anti-everything" for holding traditional values
  • The pressure to shrink your ambition, your faith, or your presence
  • The tendency to become defensive instead of constructive
  • Choosing to stay in your lane, build something solid, and not get pulled into the culture war
  • Keeping God first in everything—for yourself and your household—as the countercultural move
Day 17 · Thursday May 22

Excel In Business

Entrepreneurial
Summary

Excellence requires the extra 20% effort. Most men want excellent lives on 80% effort, but the Pareto Principle proves otherwise: the top 20% of effort produces 80% of the momentum. This separates the great from the average. After performing a task, ask yourself: "Can I do it better?" Find one thing you can improve and do it. Build the habit of pushing through average and into excellence.

Verse

Whatever your hand finds to do, do it with all your might.

Ecclesiastes 9:10
Discussion Question

Think of something you did recently at work or at home. Where was there a moment you settled for 80% when you knew you had more to give?

  • The email you sent without a second read because you were in a hurry
  • The proposal or project you shipped that felt like it could use one more pass
  • Delegating something you actually should have done yourself
  • Using a template when the situation deserved a custom approach
  • The pitch you gave that was good, but not personal, so it missed the connection
  • Consistency in the framework, but abandoning the personal touch to save time
  • The difference between grinding through calls and actually connecting with each one
Day 18 · Friday May 23

Generational Conversations

Gallant
Summary

You need to hear the stories of seasoned men and impart wisdom to younger men. Honor older men by listening to their stories—really listening. Be an elder brother to younger men, not their father. A Gallant man sits under those ahead of him with genuine curiosity, and he leans into those behind him with patience. The conversation itself is the gift. His story, honestly told and carefully heard, is the inheritance.

Verse

Don't be harsh or impatient with an older man. Talk to him as you would your own father, and to the younger men as your brothers.

1 Timothy 5:1
Discussion Question

Who is an older man in your life whose story you haven't fully heard, and what's been stopping you from sitting down with him?

  • A mentor, leader, or role model you've never actually asked about their journey
  • The specific question that would open the real story: "What would you tell yourself 10 years ago?"
  • Sitting with intentional time and a notebook, not a hallway conversation
  • Curiosity about how he kept faith first, stayed composed, managed bandwidth
  • Listening instead of offering advice or filling the space with your own stories
  • The man who's already invested in you, so this is going back to the well, not cold outreach
  • Naming what you actually want to learn from him, and being specific enough that it matters
Day 19 · Saturday May 24

Control Your Temper

Faithful
Summary

Anger digs deep into the soul. You need to distinguish between the four types—Rage, Fury, Indignation, and Wrath—and understand that anger can have a role if your motivations are pure and your response is disciplined. The real battle isn't managing anger once it's hot. It's consecrating yourself daily so you're already in position for God to grab you when frustration builds. Daily devotion is the anchor. Ongoing conversation with God throughout the day is the lifeline.

Verse

In your anger do not sin: Do not let the sun go down while you are still angry.

Ephesians 4:26
Discussion Question

Which of the four faces of anger—rage, fury, indignation, or wrath—do you find yourself most prone to, and how has that shown up in your relationships?

  • Frustrated expectations leading to hurtful words, once a week you implode
  • You know prayer redirects it, but you default to the explosion first
  • The gap between knowing what to do and actually doing it in the moment
  • Morning devotion is solid, but keeping the conversation ongoing all day is the real work
  • The pause where the flesh negotiates with the Spirit—that's where the decision lives
  • Consecration throughout the day, not just at the start: ongoing conversation, not ritual
  • When frustration builds, remembering you're already in conversation with God. Just keep talking.
Day 20 · Sunday May 25

Invest In A Child

Philanthropic
Summary

A philanthropic man is heroic in defense of the weak and fatherless. Specifically, by investing in children trapped in systemic poverty. This could be a monthly sponsorship that introduces a kid to the gospel, funds education, and meets physical and emotional needs. It could be serving in a local program. The point is consistent, intentional investment in a child's life outside your own household. Look into it. Make the commitment real.

Verse

Defend the cause of the weak and fatherless; maintain the rights of the poor and oppressed.

Psalm 82:3
Discussion Question

What would it practically take for you to commit to consistently investing in a child outside your own household?

  • A child sponsorship through a faith-based organization, monthly and consistent
  • Being an active participant, not passive (reading letters together, discussing impact)
  • Wanting to see concrete results: is she getting better education, better nutrition?
  • A local program, mentorship, tutoring, or direct relationship with a kid who needs investment
  • The commitment mattering more than the amount of money
  • Making it something you do with your spouse, not something they carry alone
  • Knowing that consistency and presence matter more than perfection or purity of impact
Day 21 · Monday May 26

The Secret Place

Adventurous
Summary

Isolation isn't something to avoid. It's where you encounter yourself and God. The wilderness exposes fears, reveals character, quiets the noise, and expands faith. Jesus was led into the wilderness. So will you be. Five key lessons: isolation is intimidating, quiet, revealing, expansive, and tests who you really are. Get out into wild space alone. Camp, hike, canoe, climb, whatever. Spend genuine time in solitude and let yourself be changed by it.

Verse

Jesus was led by the Spirit into the wilderness.

Luke 4:1
Discussion Question

When was the last time you were truly alone in silence, and what did that either reveal or expose about where you are right now?

  • A hiking trip, camping trip, or canoe outing where you're genuinely alone
  • What comes up when the screens are off, the schedule is clear, and nobody knows where you are
  • The fears that surface, the clarity that emerges, the character that gets tested
  • Why you avoid it (busyness, distraction, fear of what you might face alone)
  • What God is trying to tell you that requires silence to hear
  • The difference between rest and revelation: both matter, but this is about revelation
  • Coming back changed, not just rested. Encounter, not escape.

How can we pray for you this week?
